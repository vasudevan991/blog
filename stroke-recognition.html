A stroke happens when blood flow to a part of the brain is blocked or a blood vessel bursts. The brain is deprived of oxygen, and within minutes, damage begins.

Though more common in older adults, stroke can happen in young people too ‚Äî especially with risk factors like:

  • High blood pressure
  • Diabetes
  • Smoking and alcohol
  • Obesity and sedentary lifestyle
  • Family history of stroke

While knowing how to recognize a stroke is crucial, preventing one is even better. Many strokes are preventable through:

  • Managing blood pressure: Keep it under 140/90 mmHg
  • Controlling diabetes: Maintain healthy blood sugar levels
  • Quitting smoking: Reduces stroke risk by up to 50%
  • Regular exercise: At least 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly
  • Healthy diet: Low in salt, rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Limiting alcohol: No more than 1-2 drinks per day
